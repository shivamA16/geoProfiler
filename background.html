Background

geo-

graphic


pro-

filing

simply put, geographic profiling is a technique that looks at a serial criminal's activities through a geographical perspective.


instead of profiling a criminal based on their behaviour, past, and characteristics, we are profiling an individual geographically - based on locations of their past crimes to determine their base of operations.

Key concepts

Journey-to-Crime

Offenders will in general travel limited distances to commit their crimes, i.e. they won't go too far from their base

Rational Choice

Offenders want to divert attention from their base but they also want to travel minimal distances to commit crimes

Crime pattern

Crime sites and opportunities are not random. They are influenced by the crimnal's mental map of spatial surroundings

Rossmo's formula

The formula developed by Dr. Kim Rossmo is the fundamental calculation used in geographic profiling today.


A grid a overlaid onto the map, and the formula is applied to each square to get the probability of that square being the base of the offender's operations.
